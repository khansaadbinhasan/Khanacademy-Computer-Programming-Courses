Beet Pickled Devlish Eggs

  • Time: 2hrs
  • Serves: 24 halves
Ingredients Quantity
serrano peppers, divided 2
water 3 cups
white vinegar 1 cup
sugar 1 1/2 cups
small beets, peeled and sliced 2
cloves garlic, crushed 3
bay leaves 3
whole black peppercorns 2 tablespoons
kosher salt 1 tablespoon
large hard-boiled eggs, peeled 12
mayonnaise 2 tablespoons
extra-virgin olive oil 1 tablespoon
Dijon-style mustard 1 tablespoon
Sriracha sauce 1 tablespoon
Mild curry powder or smoked paprika for garnish, (optional) -

Step 1: Cut 1 of the serrano peppers into halves lengthwise, retaining the seeds. Place the pepper in a large saucepan and add the water, vinegar, sugar, beets, garlic, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat, reduce the heat to low, and simmer until the beets are tender, about 20 minutes. Remove the pot from the heat and let cool completely. Add the eggs to the beet liquid and refrigerate for at least 6 hours. Remove the eggs from the liquid and let them dry completely. Cut the eggs into halves lengthwise and remove the yolks. Place the yolks in a medium bowl and place the whites on a large plate. Add the mayonnaise, olive oil, mustard, and Sriracha to the yolks. Mix and mash to combine thoroughly. Fill the whites with the yolk mixture. Slice the remaining serrano pepper into rounds. Top each egg with a slice of serrano and a sprinkle of curry powder, if desired.

Step 2: Add the eggs to the beet liquid and refrigerate for at least 6 hours. Remove the eggs from the liquid and let them dry completely.

Step 3: Cut the eggs into halves lengthwise and remove the yolks. Place the yolks in a medium bowl and place the whites on a large plate. Add the mayonnaise, olive oil, mustard, and Sriracha to the yolks. Mix and mash to combine thoroughly. Fill the whites with the yolk mixture. Slice the remaining serrano pepper into rounds. Top each egg with a slice of serrano and a sprinkle of curry powder, if desired.

Fettuccine Bolognese

  • Time: 5hrs
  • Serves: 4 to 6
Ingredients Quantity
Salt and pepper As per taste
coarsely ground pork shoulder 1 pound
coarsely ground veal 1 pound
coarsely ground lamb 1 pound
ground cloves -
chicken livers, finely pureed 1/2 cup
fresh sage leaves, minced 1/4 cup
Fresh Basil leaves, chopped 1/4 cup
fresh parsley leaves, chopped 1/4 cup
chili flakes 1 teaspoon
large onion, finely diced 1
celery, finely diced 1 stalk
large carrot, finely diced 1
red wine 1/2 cup
chicken stock 6 cups
veal stock 6 cups
whole peeled tomatoes, crushed by hand 32 oz
bay leaf 1
stick butter 1/2
heavy cream 1 cup
Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, grated 1 cup
pasta, cooked right before serving 1 1/2 pounds
torn basil leaves and Parmigiano-Reggiano, for serving -

Step 1: Heat olive oil in a large rondeau or skillet with straight sides over high heat until smoking.

Step 2: Season the ground meats with salt, pepper, and ground cloves. Add the pork, veal, and lamb, and cook, stirring occasionally and breaking up large chunks of meat until cooked through, about 15 minutes.

Step 3: Once meats are caramelized, add the pureed chicken livers and chopped sage, basil, chili flakes and parsley and cook, stirring constantly, until just cooked through, about 2-4 minutes.

Step 4:Add onion, celery, and carrots and cook, stirring frequently. Now deglaze with the wine to the meats and bring to a boil over high heat. Allow to reduce until almost gone, about 15 minutes.

Step 5:Add chicken stock, veal stock, bay leaf, and tomatoes. Bring pot to a boil, reduce to a bare simmer, and cook, stirring occasionally, until flavors are married and sauce is reduced to a thick, spoon-coating consistency, about 2 hours.

Step 6:Finish by adding some butter and heavy cream. Bring to a boil and season with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the sauce to cool, then refrigerate at least overnight.

Step 7:For serving, heat amount needed in sauté pan and add some heavy cream, chicken stock and black pepper. Fold in basil, Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese and parsley and serve with desired pasta. Garnish with torn basil leaves and Parmigiano-Reggiano.

Taiwanese Three Cup Chicken

  • Time: 1hr
  • Serves: 4 to 6
Ingredients Quantity
Salt and pepper As per taste
sesame oil 3/4 cup
fresh ginger, peeled and sliced into 12 to 15 thick discs 1 3-inch piece
whole cloves garlic 12 to 15
scallions, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces 4 whole
small fresh red chilies, halved or sliced 2 to 3
rice wine 1 cup
light soy sauce 1 cup
sugar 2 tablespoons
packed fresh Thai basil leaves 2 cups

Step 1: Heat the sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.

Step 2: Add the ginger, garlic, scallions, and chilies, and cook until just fragrant, about 1 minute.

Step 3: Add the chicken pieces and lightly brown, stirring occasionally, 2 to 3 minutes.

Step 4:Add the rice wine and soy sauce and bring to a boil.

Step 5:Reduce to a simmer and cook, uncovered, until the chicken pieces are cooked through and the sauce has slightly reduced, about 15 minutes.

Step 6:Stir in the sugar until just dissolved.

Step 7:Remove from the heat and stir in the basil. Serve immediately.
